Historical Places

City Hall

  • Nelson Mandela gave a speech from the balcony of City Hall immediately upon his release from prison, on 11 February 1990.
  • City Hall, as its name suggests, was built to function as the centre of city administration and as such housed the offices of the City of Cape Town.
  • It remains an imposing building, helped in no small part by a restoration of its façade in 1979.

Church Square

  • The square is surrounded by architecturally impressive buildings and provides a surprisingly quiet space in the heart of Cape Town
  • The only part of the original church still standing is the church tower. When it became unstable, Groote Kerk was rebuilt on the same site between 1813 and 1841.
  • Between 1730 and 1753 Church Square became a meeting place for local dogs

Green Market Square

  • Green Market Square was first established in 1696, in the first century of Dutch occupation of the Cape colony.
  • It’s the second oldest public space in Cape Town, after the Grand Parade
  • This is one of the best places in Cape Town to buy curios and gifts for your loved ones back home, while supporting the entrepreneurial traders.
